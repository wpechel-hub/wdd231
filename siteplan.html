Salt Lake City Chamber of Commerce

Website Site Plan

Site Name

Salt Lake City Chamber of Commerce

Site Purpose

This website will serve as a resource hub for businesses in Salt Lake City, Utah. The site will provide information on local events, host networking opportunities, create a searchable business directory, and encourage local commerce. It aims to promote economic growth in the Salt Lake Valley, support local businesses of all sizes, and foster a sense of community among business owners and residents.

Target Market

Business owners, entrepreneurs, and patrons in Salt Lake City and the greater Wasatch Front region, including visitors and new residents considering relocation.

Site Goals

  • Improve member engagement by providing valuable resources and networking opportunities unique to the Salt Lake City business community.
  • Attract new businesses and encourage economic development across all Salt Lake City neighborhoods.
  • Improve the visibility and reputation of the chamber as a trusted authority in local business matters.
  • Connect residents and visitors with the diverse businesses and services available in Salt Lake City.

Scenarios

  1. A local restaurant owner is interested in joining the chamber to network with other Salt Lake City business owners. They visit the website to find information on membership benefits, fees, and how to apply online.
  2. A Salt Lake City resident is looking for upcoming events and workshops hosted by the chamber. They visit the website to browse the events calendar and register for activities relevant to their industry.
  3. A family considering relocating from out of state wants to learn more about Salt Lake City's business environment, quality of life, and community resources. They explore the chamber's website for business directories, demographic information, and community highlights.

SEO Plan

  • Use relevant keywords in page titles, meta descriptions, headings, and body content (e.g., "Salt Lake City businesses," "SLC chamber of commerce," "Utah business networking").
  • Verify the site on Google's Business Profile.
  • Pursue inbound links and backlinks from member business websites to improve search rankings.
  • Embed Google Analytics on all site pages to track traffic and user behavior.
  • Ensure all images include descriptive alt attributes to support accessibility and image search indexing.
